Attacker shouting 'Allahu Akbar' stabs Jewish man in NYC

Upper West Side stabbing investigated as possible hate crime

A man shouting "Allahu Akbar" stabbed two people, including a Jewish man, with a screwdriver on New York City's Upper West Side, according to police reports. The NYPD has taken a suspect into custody and is investigating the incident as a possible hate crime.

Details of the attack

The attack occurred near Central Park, with witnesses reporting the assailant yelled the Arabic phrase before striking. One victim was identified as Jewish, though police have not confirmed whether both victims were targeted for their religion.

NYPD response and suspect in custody

Police responded quickly to the scene and apprehended the alleged attacker. The NYPD's Hate Crimes Task Force has joined the investigation, though officials caution that motives are still being determined.

Recent pattern of antisemitic incidents

This attack follows a troubling rise in antisemitic incidents in New York City, with reported cases increasing by over 300% since October 2023 according to NYPD statistics. Community leaders have called for increased protection for Jewish residents.

Community reactions and security concerns

Local Jewish organizations expressed alarm about the attack, with some calling for enhanced security at synagogues and community centers. The incident has reignited debates about public safety and hate crime enforcement in the city.

Mayor's statement

Mayor Eric Adams condemned the violence, stating: "New York City has zero tolerance for hate of any kind. We will prosecute this attack to the fullest extent of the law."

Legal implications and potential charges

The suspect could face multiple felony charges including assault and potentially hate crime enhancements, which carry stiffer penalties under New York law. Prosecutors are reviewing surveillance footage and witness statements.

What we know about the NYC screwdriver attack

The attack appears to have been unprovoked and targeted strangers. The use of a screwdriver as a weapon suggests an improvised weapon rather than premeditation, though investigators are still determining whether the assailant came prepared to attack.
