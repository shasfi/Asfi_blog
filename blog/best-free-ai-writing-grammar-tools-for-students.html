1. Grammarly

Grammarly is a widely used AI-powered writing assistant that helps students improve their grammar, spelling, and clarity. It integrates seamlessly with browsers, Microsoft Office, and other platforms, making it ideal for essays and research papers. A standout feature is its tone detector, which ensures your writing matches the intended style.

Best for: Real-time grammar and style corrections

Grammarly is perfect for students who need instant feedback on their writing. Its free version covers basic grammar and spelling checks, while premium offers advanced suggestions.

2. Hemingway Editor

The Hemingway Editor highlights complex sentences, passive voice, and adverbs to make your writing clearer and more concise. It’s a great tool for students who want to improve readability. The desktop app allows offline use, which is handy for focused writing sessions.

Key feature: Readability score

Hemingway provides a readability score, helping students tailor their writing to specific grade levels or audiences.

3. ProWritingAid

ProWritingAid offers in-depth writing reports, including grammar, style, and readability checks. It’s ideal for students working on long-form assignments like theses or dissertations. The free version includes a limited number of reports per day.

Best for: Detailed writing analysis

Students who need comprehensive feedback on their writing will benefit from ProWritingAid’s detailed suggestions and explanations.

4. QuillBot

QuillBot is an AI-powered paraphrasing tool that helps students rephrase sentences while retaining the original meaning. It’s useful for avoiding plagiarism and improving sentence structure. The free version includes basic paraphrasing options.

Key feature: Synonym slider

QuillBot’s synonym slider allows students to adjust the level of rewriting, making it flexible for different needs.

5. LanguageTool

LanguageTool is an open-source grammar checker that supports multiple languages, making it ideal for multilingual students. It checks for grammar, style, and punctuation errors. The free version covers basic checks.

Best for: Multilingual support

LanguageTool is perfect for students who write in multiple languages or need grammar checks beyond English.

6. Scribbr

Scribbr offers a free plagiarism checker and citation generator, essential for academic writing. It helps students ensure their work is original and properly cited. The plagiarism checker is limited to small documents in the free version.

Key feature: Citation generator

Scribbr’s citation generator supports various styles like APA, MLA, and Chicago, saving students time on formatting.

7. Ginger

Ginger is a grammar and spell checker with a unique sentence rephrasing feature. It’s great for students who want to improve their writing fluency. The free version includes basic corrections.

Best for: Sentence rephrasing

Ginger’s rephrasing tool helps students find better ways to express their ideas, enhancing clarity and coherence.

8. DeepL Write

DeepL Write is an AI-powered tool that suggests improvements for grammar, style, and word choice. It’s particularly useful for non-native English speakers. The free version offers basic suggestions.

Key feature: Context-aware corrections

DeepL Write provides context-aware suggestions, making it more accurate than many other tools.

Which One Should You Choose

For general grammar and spelling, Grammarly or LanguageTool are excellent choices. If you need readability improvements, try Hemingway Editor. For paraphrasing, QuillBot is ideal, while Scribbr is best for plagiarism and citations. Choose based on your specific needs and writing goals.
