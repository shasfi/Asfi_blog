About

What AsfiBlog is, and how it's made.

AsfiBlog is an independent publication that tracks the stories getting the most attention each day across technology, business, science, and health — then breaks each one down in clear, plain-English language so readers don't have to dig through jargon to understand what happened and why it matters.

We started AsfiBlog on a simple idea: the biggest stories of the day are often buried under corporate press releases, paywalls, or fifteen-paragraph articles that take five minutes to say what could be said in two. Our job is to find what people are actually searching for and explain it clearly, quickly, and honestly.

What we cover

Every dispatch falls into one of four desks:

  • Technology — product launches, AI shifts, and platform news that actually changes how people work or live.
  • Business — markets, deals, and workplace shifts, explained without the finance-textbook language.
  • Science — new research and discoveries, translated for a reader who isn't a specialist.
  • Health — trending wellness topics checked against what the actual science says.

How our stories are put together

AsfiBlog uses an AI-assisted editorial workflow: trending topics are identified from public news sources, then drafted with the help of AI language models and paired with relevant imagery. Every published piece is meant to inform, not replace, the original reporting it's based on — we always encourage readers to explore primary sources for the full picture. See our Disclaimer for more detail on this process.

Our editorial standards

Even with an automated pipeline, we hold every article to the same bar: no invented facts, no misleading headlines, and no recycled paragraphs lifted from other outlets. Sources are chosen from established news wires, and any figure, quote, or statistic used in an article is meant to reflect what was actually reported.

Founder & Editor

Sheikh Asfi (Asfand Yar Ali) is a full-stack software developer specializing in Laravel/PHP and AI-integrated web applications, currently completing a BS in Software Engineering. AsfiBlog was built as an independent project combining his interest in automation, AI tooling, and publishing — from the trend-detection pipeline to the site itself.
